## Why X Matters for Pear

LinkedIn is where managers and team leads discover tools. **X is where indie hackers, open source devs, and AI power users live.** These are pear's earliest adopters — developers who already use Claude Code, Cursor, and Aider daily and feel the learning gap firsthand.

X is also where developer tools go viral. Bolt.new went from one tweet to $40M ARR. Cursor's founders posted for years before launch. Every major dev tool breakout in 2024-2026 started on X.

The current plan treats X as a cross-posting channel with 9 posts over 13 weeks. That's not enough. This strategy makes X a first-class channel with its own voice, cadence, and growth tactics.

## The 2026 X Algorithm

What you need to know:

- **Grok handles ranking** (since Jan 2026) — distribution model has fundamentally shifted
- **Replies are the #1 signal** — a tweet with 20 replies beats one with 100 likes
- **First 30 minutes determine everything** — engagement velocity triggers algorithmic push
- **Positive/constructive content gets boosted** — negative content gets throttled even with high engagement
- **No external links** — zero median engagement for non-Premium accounts. Links kill reach.
- **Native video gets ~10x engagement** vs text-only
- **Threads get 3x engagement** vs single tweets
- **Long mega-threads (15+) are dead** — sweet spot is 3-7 tweets

## Growth Phases

### Phase 1: Reply-First (Weeks 1-4, target 0 → 300 followers)

**80% of your time on X should be replying, not posting.**

At 0 followers, your posts go to nobody. But your replies appear under tweets with thousands of views. One good reply on a viral tweet drives more profile visits than 5 of your own posts.

**The system:**

1. **Build a target list of 30-50 accounts** in these categories:

   | Category | Example Accounts | Why |
   |----------|-----------------|-----|
   | AI coding tools | @cursor_ai, @ClaudeCode, @aaborovkov (Aider), @stackaborovkov | Your direct ecosystem |
   | AI companies | @AnthropicAI, @OpenAI, @xaborovkov | High-visibility conversations |
   | Indie hackers | @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @dannypostmaa | Your tribe |
   | Dev educators | @ThePrimeagen, @t3dotgg, @firaborovkov | Adjacent audience |
   | Build-in-public | @tdinh_me, @panaborovkov, @yaborovkov | Mutual support network |
   | AI critics/thinkers | @kaborovkov, people posting "AI is making devs worse" takes | Your thesis allies |

2. **Turn on notifications** for the top 15 accounts

3. **Reply within 30 minutes** of their posts — early replies get 10-50x more impressions than late ones

4. **Reply quality rules:**
   - Add genuine insight, a personal experience, or a specific follow-up question
   - Never: "Great post!", "This 🔥", "So true" — these are invisible
   - Show your thinking, not your agreement
   - Reference your own experience building pear when it's genuinely relevant (not forced)

5. **Daily commitment:** 30 minutes minimum replying. Track with a simple tally — aim for 20-30 replies/day.

**Example good replies:**

> Someone posts about Claude Code being fast but hard to learn from:
> "This is exactly why I'm building pear — a CLI that injects teaching context into your AI responses. The speed is real but the learning gap is real too. I've shipped 4 products with AI and still catch myself not understanding my own code."

> Someone posts frustration debugging AI-generated code:
> "Spent 3 hours yesterday debugging code Claude wrote for me in 30 seconds. The ROI math on AI coding tools breaks down completely when you don't understand what was generated. We need better tooling for the comprehension layer."

> Someone shares a hot take on vibe coding:
> "The vibe coders who survive will be the ones who eventually learn what their code does. The ones who don't will hit a wall when the first non-trivial bug shows up. I've watched it happen to 3 founders in the last month."

### Phase 2: Content + Engagement (Weeks 5-8, target 300 → 700 followers)

Now you have some followers and social proof. Shift to 50/50 posting and replying.

**Posting cadence:** 5x/week minimum (Mon-Fri)

**Content mix:**
- 40% — Observations and takes (specific, from your experience)
- 30% — Build-in-public updates (screenshots, terminal recordings, metrics)
- 20% — Educational (short, punchy insights about code/AI/learning)
- 10% — Product (pear updates, waitlist, demos)

**Format hierarchy (what performs best → worst):**
1. Short threads (3-5 tweets) with a screenshot or video
2. Single tweet + native image/GIF/video
3. Single tweet with a strong hook and specific take
4. Cross-posted LinkedIn thread (adapted, not copy-pasted)

### Phase 3: Compounding (Weeks 9-13, target 700 → 1,500+)

Your replies are getting noticed. Your content is getting some traction. Now it compounds.

- Increase posting to daily
- Start doing X Spaces with other indie builders (even small ones — 5-10 listeners is fine)
- Identify your superfans (people who consistently engage) and DM them beta access
- Post demo clips of pear (15-30 seconds, no intro, just action)
- Share real metrics publicly ("14 users. $0 MRR. 3 people told me this changed how they code.")

## X-Native Content Rules

X is NOT LinkedIn. Don't cross-post directly. Adapt.

| LinkedIn | X |
|----------|---|
| 1,300-1,600 characters | Under 280 chars per tweet (or short thread) |
| Professional, polished narrative | Conversational, punchy, opinionated |
| "Here's what I learned..." | "Hot take:" or "Nobody talks about this:" |
| Ends with open questions | Ends with a strong statement or a provocative question |
| No emojis (or minimal) | Emojis OK when natural (not decorative) |
| Links in first comment | No links ever (kills reach) |
| Stories with arc | One idea per tweet, hit hard |

**Voice on X:** You're the guy at the bar explaining something interesting, not the guy on stage giving a talk. Shorter sentences. More personality. Occasional humor. Strong opinions held loosely.

## What NOT to Do

1. **Don't post and disappear** — if you're not engaging in the first 30 min after posting, you're wasting the post
2. **Don't link out** — no "read more on my blog" or "sign up at pearcode.dev" in tweets. Put links in your bio only. When someone asks, say "link in bio"
3. **Don't copy-paste LinkedIn posts** — the voice is wrong, the length is wrong, the format is wrong
4. **Don't buy followers** — algorithm penalizes low engagement-to-follower ratios
5. **Don't automate replies** — Grok can detect bot patterns. Manual only.
6. **Don't spread yourself thin** — better to reply to 20 accounts consistently than 100 accounts sporadically
7. **Don't be self-deprecating about your size** — "sharing with my audience of 0" invites pity, not follows. Confidence attracts.
8. **Don't post generic advice** — "the best way to learn is by building" is true but invisible. Specific experience beats universal wisdom.

## The One Thing That Matters Most

**Consistency beats virality.** The people who win on X are still posting at month 6. Most quit at month 2 when they're at 200 followers and feel like nobody's listening.

You're not building an audience. You're building recognition. The 50th time someone sees your name in a reply thread, they finally click your profile. That's how X works at the early stage.

Show up every day. Reply with substance. Post with specificity. The compounding kicks in around month 3.
